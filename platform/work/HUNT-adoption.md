## Synthesis ‚Äî the 3 highest-leverage adoption closures

1. **AD-1 (Chart FieldConfig authoring-lag) ‚Äî the single biggest stranded value.** The runner already renders the *entire* Grafana FieldConfig/FieldOverride/Threshold system for every chart type; the Constructor exposes **none of it** for charts (gauge got thresholds; chart didn't). This is the widest "authoring surface lags render surface" gap in the platform, on the dominant panel. **HYBRID recommended:** author the vital-few now (a shared `colorMode` + `thresholds` control lifted from gauge, wired into the chart schema ‚Äî closes ~80% of the value), and defer the per-series `FieldOverride` sub-editor behind its real trigger (a genuine multi-series threshold config). This respects YAGNI on the complex override matrix while un-stranding the common case.

2. **AD-2 (saveGuard ‚üÇ describeApp) ‚Äî cheap, high-integrity.** The manifest is the declared SSOT of "what exists," but the save gate never uses it as an authority, so un-renderable configs save clean (fail-late). One additional `capability-compat` check turns describeApp() from a *description* into a *contract* at the authoring boundary ‚Äî the Builder.io content-model discipline the platform already aspires to (Law 4). Small effort, protects every future save and every third-party plugin.

3. **AD-3 (PropSchemaForm stranded + duplicated `isVisible`) ‚Äî kill the fork or the primitive.** The platform maintains **two** schema‚Üíform renderers with **two** hand-synced visibility/validation cores (`showWhen.ts:9` admits the mirror). This is live DRY debt with shotgun-surgery risk. Not a "wire it up" ‚Äî a **decision**: extract the shared field-engine so both compose it, or demote PropSchemaForm to the external-embedder primitive it claims to be. Either way, one `isVisible` semantics, one place.

**HYBRID verdict:** only **AD-1** genuinely warrants a hybrid (adopt the common FieldConfig fields, defer the FieldOverride matrix). AD-2 and AD-3 are clean single-direction closures. AD-4 is the one to resolve by *demotion* rather than adoption unless a multi-target panel is real ‚Äî the batch lane on the port is a YAGNI tax until then. AD-5 and AD-6 are small, honest completions of capabilities the platform already paid for on one side.

**Confidence: 0.82.** The 0.18: AD-3/AD-4/AD-5/AD-6 sit in Lane A (`packages/react`) or Lane B (`core`, uncommitted) hot trees ‚Äî re-verify the file:lines on a quiesced tree before acting. All findings were confirmed by reading the consumer site (not just the definition), so the *direction* is solid even if a line number drifts.
