**Always:** Sonnet composes the full brief (Pre-Work Gate, Decision Inventory, constraints, context) at its own quality standard. This is the **amplify** half of relay — the mediator grasps the intent and develops it to a complete, agent-ready form (implied standard, entailed constraints, needed context), *traceable to the verbatim words below, never substituting them*. Preserve ∧ amplify, not preserve-only and not rewrite. Full rule: `01-team-and-decisions.md` §B.

**Verbatim block is the DEFAULT — not opt-in.** Whenever the user's message carries task-bearing content (intent, a constraint, a decision, a correction), the brief includes the user's exact words as a verbatim block **beside** Sonnet's structured brief — never instead of it. This is the structural cure for the mediator's core failure mode ("what I wrote isn't carried over" — the user's words don't reach the agent): preserve, never replace, so the paraphrase is always checkable against the original.

```
User directive (verbatim):
"[user's exact words here, unchanged — typos and all]"
```

Both are present: Sonnet's structured analysis + the user's verbatim words. If Sonnet's framing diverges from the verbatim block, the divergence is visible — and the verbatim block wins.

**"Relay it" (or similar)** only raises this from default to mandatory + signals the words are the primary payload (relay them first, frame second).

**Why:** User: "when I tell you something, your analysis should be there AND you must relay mine" — both together. Standing discipline: `01-team-and-decisions.md` (A intake, B relay, D back-relay).
