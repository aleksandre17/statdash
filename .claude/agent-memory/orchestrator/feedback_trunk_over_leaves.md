**Rule:** the owner's standing mandate is the **big systemic architecture overhaul** (every element → a self-owning NODE that declares its Bounded-Element contract once, every surface a generic projection — ADR-038 implemented SYSTEMICALLY, Strangler-Fig). Tactical point-fixes (CORS, one inspector Summary-Card, one ChartSchema drain) are **leaves** — necessary + visible, but NEVER a substitute for the trunk. Do not let a stream of green leaf-wins convince me (or read to the owner) that the mandate is being met.

**Why (2026-07-11):** after I delivered CORS + Move 1 + a ChartSchema-drain, the owner cut in — *"didn't we say we'd do the big overhaul of nodes and architectures? what are you doing about THAT?"* I had gone tactical: fixing leaves while the root (unify the object model, cut the per-type special-casing at its source, migrate every node to declare-its-contract) sat untouched. He wants the ideologue/architect driving the foundational program, not an executor clearing a leaf-backlog. Recurs with [[built-but-buried-audit]], [[activate-not-shadow]], [[lead-is-idea-source]] — same root: think trunk, be foundational, don't mistake motion for direction.

**How to apply:** (1) When a session fills with leaf-fixes, periodically re-surface the trunk: "these are leaves of the object-model overhaul — here's where the trunk stands." (2) Frame every leaf as a slice of the systemic program (the ChartSchema drain = a pilot of Bounded-Element, not a standalone win). (3) Keep an architect/platform-architect driving the systemic backbone in parallel with leaf work — the trunk should never be idle while I clear leaves. (4) In status reports, lead with the trunk's state, not the leaf count. (5) A visible fix is not the mandate; the mandate is the architecture. Good ≠ best; motion ≠ direction.
